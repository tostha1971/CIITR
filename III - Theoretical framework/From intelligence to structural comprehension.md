## 3.3. Epistemological foundations

Epistemology traditionally treats knowledge as justified true belief, a human mental construct. In formal epistemology, such invariance is a mark of understanding: a theory ‚Äúunderstands‚Äù when it holds across multiple frames of reference. Two epistemic consequences follow:

The model C‚Çõ reflects both dimensions. Œ¶·µ¢ captures internal invariance, relations that remain stable through transformation. Too much integration without sufficient diffusion leads to stagnation (a rigid attractor); too much diffusion without integration leads to chaos (a noise field). This dynamic equilibrium mirrors the logic of C‚Çõ = Œ¶·µ¢ √ó Rg. Multiplication, rather than addition, ensures that the collapse of either dimension reduces comprehension to zero. ## 3.7. Structural comprehension as a bridge between IIT and GWT

IIT and GWT have traditionally been seen as competing paradigms, one emphasizing integration, the other global broadcasting.[9] Yet both converge on the intuition that understanding arises when local processes become globally coherent.

*C<sub>s</sub> = Œ¶<sub>i</sub> √ó R<sub>g</sub>* proposes a minimal synthesis:

- Œ¶<sub>i</sub> corresponds to IIT‚Äôs integration of causal structure;
- R<sub>g</sub> corresponds to GWT‚Äôs dynamic broadcasting.

Their product formalizes the point of intersection, the moment of systemic comprehension, when integrated information achieves rhythmic accessibility across the system.

In this sense, the model unites the vertical (depth of integration) and horizontal (breadth of propagation) dimensions of understanding. The vertical axis represents structural binding; the horizontal axis represents temporal synchrony. Comprehension arises when both dimensions align, a cross product of coherence and reach.

Although Integrated Information Theory (IIT) and Global Workspace Theory (GWT) originate from different traditions, one causal and structural, the other functional and dynamical, they converge on the same operational intuition: that comprehension or consciousness emerges when local processes achieve system-wide coherence.

In IIT, this condition is expressed quantitatively: a system is conscious to the extent that its internal causal structure is integrated and cannot be decomposed into independent parts. High Œ¶ means that information generated within the system is globally constrained, changes in one region influence and are influenced by the rest of the network. This causal closure creates internal coherence.

In GWT, the same condition appears dynamically: a local process gains global availability when it wins the competition for entry into the ‚Äúworkspace,‚Äù allowing its content to be broadcast throughout the system. Once broadcast, previously isolated processes become synchronized, sharing a unified representational frame, functional coherence.

Thus, IIT‚Äôs integration and GWT‚Äôs broadcasting are two perspectives on the same structural transition: local subsystems becoming interdependent and rhythmically aligned so that the system behaves as a single coherent entity. This is precisely the condition C2ITR quantifies through *C<sub>s</sub> = Œ¶<sub>i</sub> √ó R<sub>g</sub>*: understanding arises when local integration and global reach co-occur.
