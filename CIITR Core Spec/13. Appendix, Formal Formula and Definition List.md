## 13. Appendix, Formal Formula and Definition List

This appendix consolidates the kernel-level formal bindings into a single audit surface. Redundancy is intentional. The purpose is retrieval, traceability, and protocol-level closure. All formal expressions are contained exclusively within the tabular blocks below.

### A1. Definitions (alphabetical, primitive and derived)

| Symbol | Type | Kernel binding | Formal binding / admissible values |
|---|---|---|---|
| $\mathrm{Accounted}_\Pi(X)$ | Derived (accounting predicate) | Predicate stating that an accounting object $X$ is defined under $\Pi$ with declared boundary and provenance. | $X \in \{E_\Pi(S),\Sigma_\Pi(S)\}$<br>$\mathrm{Accounted}_\Pi(X)\in\{0,1\}$ |
| $\mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(\theta,S)$ | Derived (admissibility predicate) | Protocol-scoped admissibility predicate for a kernel target $\theta$ asserted for system $S$ under protocol $\Pi$. | $\theta \in \{\Phi_i,R_g\}$<br>$\mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(\theta,S)\in\{0,1,\varnothing\}$ |
| $\mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(\theta,S,t)$ | Derived (time-indexed admissibility predicate) | Time-indexed admissibility predicate used when admissibility is evaluated over temporally extended evidence under $\Pi$. | $\theta \in \{\Phi_i,R_g\}$<br>$t \in \mathcal{T}_\Pi$<br>$\mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(\theta,S,t)\in\{0,1,\varnothing\}$ |
| $C_s$ | Derived (classification) | Structural comprehension classification of system $S$ under protocol $\Pi$ and observable set $\mathcal{O}$. | $C_s \;=\; C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O})$<br>$C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O}) \in \{0,1,\varnothing\}$ |
| $\mathrm{CertClaim}_\Pi(S)$ | Derived (governance predicate) | Predicate denoting that a certification-grade non-null claim is being asserted under $\Pi$. | $\mathrm{CertClaim}_\Pi(S)=1 \;:\Longleftrightarrow\; \big(C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O})=1\big)\,\vee\,\big(\mathrm{MVR}_\Pi(S)=1\big)\,\vee\,\big(\mathrm{Nash\text{-}CIITR}_\Pi(S)=1\big)$ |
| $\mathrm{CertOutcome}_\Pi(S)$ | Derived (governance output) | Certification outcome class under $\Pi$. | $\mathrm{CertOutcome}_\Pi(S) \in \{\mathrm{CERTIFIED},\mathrm{NOT\_CERTIFIED},\mathrm{UNDEFINED}\}$ |
| $\mathrm{CPJ}_\Pi(S)$ | Derived (constraint functional) | Comprehension-per-joule constraint functional, used as an admissibility gate for certification-relevant non-null claims under declared scalar proxies and energy accounting. | $\mathrm{CPJ}_\Pi(S)=\dfrac{\widehat{\Phi}_i(S \mid \Pi)\,\widehat{R}_g(S \mid \Pi)}{E_\Pi(S)}$<br>$\neg \mathrm{Accounted}_\Pi\!\big(E_\Pi(S)\big)\;\Longrightarrow\;\mathrm{CPJ}_\Pi(S)=\varnothing$ |
| $\Delta\Pi$ | Derived (protocol perturbation element) | A protocol perturbation element belonging to an admissibility-preserving perturbation class. | $\Delta\Pi \in \mathcal{P}_\Pi$ |
| $\mathrm{Decidable}_\Pi(\theta,S)$ | Derived (protocol property) | Predicate stating that admissibility of $\theta$ for $S$ is determinable under declared observables and rules, without post hoc rule invention. | $\theta \in \{\Phi_i,R_g\}$<br>$\mathrm{Decidable}_\Pi(\theta,S)\in\{0,1\}$ |
| $\mathrm{Disq}_\Pi(S)$ | Derived (disqualification predicate) | Protocol-bound disqualification predicate with binding authority against positive classification claims. | $\mathrm{Disq}_\Pi(S)\in\{0,1\}$<br>$\mathrm{Disq}_\Pi(S)=1 \;\Longrightarrow\; C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O}) \neq 1$ |
| $\mathrm{Disq}_\Pi(S,t)$ | Derived (time-indexed disqualification predicate) | Protocol-bound disqualification predicate applied at time index $t$ in temporally extended adjudication. | $t\in\mathcal{T}_\Pi$<br>$\mathrm{Disq}_\Pi(S,t)\in\{0,1\}$ |
| $E_\Pi(S)$ | Derived (accounting object) | Protocol-accounted energy expenditure attributable to evaluation of $S$ under $\Pi$, with declared attribution boundary. | $\mathrm{Accounted}_\Pi\!\big(E_\Pi(S)\big)\in\{0,1\}$ |
| $\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{RER}}(S,t)$ | Threshold event predicate | Candidate ignition event predicate for $\mathrm{RER}$, defined by a protocol-declared candidate signature predicate. | $\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{RER}}(S,t)\;:\Longleftrightarrow\;\sigma^{\mathrm{cand}}_\Pi\!\big(\mathcal{O}_{R_g}(t)\big)=1$ |
| $\equiv_\Pi$ | Derived (observational equivalence) | Protocol-bound observational equivalence relation over time-indexed observables, used in temporal invariance bindings. | $\equiv_\Pi \subseteq \mathcal{O}_{R_g}\times \mathcal{O}_{R_g}$<br>$\mathcal{O}_{R_g}(t_a)\equiv_\Pi\mathcal{O}_{R_g}(t_b)$ |
| $\Phi_i$ | Primitive axis | Integrated relational information, treated as an admissibility object under protocol $\Pi$. | $\mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(\Phi_i,S)\in\{0,1,\varnothing\}$ |
| $\widehat{\Phi}_i(S \mid \Pi)$ | Derived (scalar proxy) | Protocol-declared scalar proxy for $\Phi_i$, admissible only if its construction is specified in $\mathcal{R}_\Pi$ and remains subject to disqualification. | $\widehat{\Phi}_i(S \mid \Pi)\in\mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}\;\cup\;\{\varnothing\}$ |
| $\mathrm{Forgery}_\Pi(S)$ | Disqualifier family (threat model) | Protocol-bound forgery predicate with disqualifying authority over positive claims. | $\mathrm{Forgery}_\Pi(S)\in\{0,1,\varnothing\}$<br>$\mathrm{Forgery}_\Pi(S)=1 \;\Longrightarrow\; C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O}) \neq 1$ |
| $\mathrm{Forgery}^{(1)}_\Pi(S,\theta)$ | Disqualifier family (first-order) | Signature conformance without kernel admissibility for target $\theta$. | $\theta\in\{\Phi_i,R_g\}$<br>$\mathrm{Forgery}^{(1)}_\Pi(S,\theta)\;:\Longleftrightarrow\;\sigma_\Pi(S,\theta)=1 \,\wedge\, \neg \mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(\theta,S)$ |
| $\mathrm{Forgery}^{(2)}_\Pi(S)$ | Disqualifier family (second-order) | Evaluation regime imitation and sensitivity to admissibility-preserving perturbations. | $\mathrm{Forgery}^{(2)}_\Pi(S)\;:\Longleftrightarrow\;\exists \Delta\Pi \in \mathcal{P}_\Pi,\;\Big(\mathrm{Pass}(S,\Pi)=1 \,\wedge\, \mathrm{Pass}(S,\Pi+\Delta\Pi)=0\Big)$ |
| $\inf(\cdot)$ | Derived (order operator) | Infimum operator over a protocol time index set, used to define first-event indices such as $t_{\mathrm{MVR}}$. | $\mathcal{T}_\Pi \text{ is treated as an ordered set sufficient for } \inf(\cdot)$ |
| $\mathrm{Invariant}_{\mathcal{P}_\Pi}(S)$ | Derived (robustness predicate) | Invariance predicate across admissibility-preserving protocol perturbations. | $\mathrm{Invariant}_{\mathcal{P}_\Pi}(S)\;:\Longleftrightarrow\;\forall \Delta\Pi \in \mathcal{P}_\Pi,\; \mathrm{MVR}_{\Pi+\Delta\Pi}(S)=1$ |
| $\mathcal{J}_\Pi(S,t)$ | Derived (joint admissibility predicate) | Joint admissibility predicate used in threshold adjudication. | $\mathcal{J}_\Pi(S,t)\;:\Longleftrightarrow\;\mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(\Phi_i,S,t)=1 \,\wedge\, \mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(R_g,S,t)=1$ |
| $M_S$ | Primitive (optional support object) | Representational manifold for system $S$, introduced only if required for definitional coherence. | $M_S \text{ is optional and protocol-scoped}$ |
| $\mathcal{M}_\Pi$ | Derived (audit metadata binder) | Protocol metadata binder required for audit closure of claims. | $\mathcal{M}_\Pi \subset \Pi$ |
| $\mathrm{MVR}_\Pi(S)$ | Threshold (classification event) | First non-null admissible classification event for $C_s$ under $\Pi$. | $\mathrm{MVR}_\Pi(S)\in\{0,1\}$<br>$\mathrm{MVR}_\Pi(S)=1 \;:\Longleftrightarrow\; t_{\mathrm{MVR}} \in \mathcal{T}_\Pi$<br>$t_{\mathrm{MVR}}=\inf\{t \in \mathcal{T}_\Pi \mid \mathcal{J}_\Pi(S,t)=1 \,\wedge\, \neg \mathrm{Disq}_\Pi(S,t)\}$ |
| $\mathrm{Nash\text{-}CIITR}_\Pi(S)$ | Threshold regime (stabilization) | Stabilized admissible regime under temporal persistence and perturbation invariance. | $\mathrm{Nash\text{-}CIITR}_\Pi(S)\in\{0,1\}$<br>$\mathrm{Nash\text{-}CIITR}_\Pi(S)=1 \;\Longleftrightarrow\; \mathrm{MVR}_\Pi(S)=1 \,\wedge\, \mathrm{Stable}_\Pi(S) \,\wedge\, \mathrm{Invariant}_{\mathcal{P}_\Pi}(S)$ |
| $\mathcal{O}$ | Primitive set (evidence) | Declared observable set, typed and provenance-bound. | $\mathcal{O}=\{o_k\}_{k=1}^{K}$<br>$o_k \in \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{adm}}$ |
| $\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{adm}}$ | Primitive set (evidence type domain) | Domain of admissible observables under the kernel. | $\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{adm}} \text{ is protocol-declared and audit-bound}$ |
| $\mathcal{O}_{\Phi}$ | Derived subset (evidence) | Declared observables relevant for $\Phi_i$ admissibility. | $\mathcal{O}_{\Phi}\subseteq\mathcal{O}$ |
| $\mathcal{O}_{R_g}$ | Derived subset (evidence) | Declared observables relevant for $R_g$ admissibility, time-indexed where required. | $\mathcal{O}_{R_g}\subseteq\mathcal{O}$<br>$\mathcal{O}_{R_g}(t)\text{ denotes time-indexed observables}$ |
| $\mathcal{P}_\Pi$ | Derived set (perturbation class) | Admissibility-preserving protocol perturbation class used to evaluate second-order forgery sensitivity. | $\mathcal{P}_\Pi \text{ is protocol-declared}$ |
| $\Pi$ | Primitive binder (protocol) | Evidence and inference regime, including negative controls and audit metadata. | $\Pi=\langle \mathcal{O},\mathcal{R}_\Pi,\Pi^{-},\mathcal{M}_\Pi\rangle$ |
| $\Pi^{-}$ | Derived (negative evidence layer) | Protocol component containing negative controls and disqualifier families. | $\Pi^{-}\subset \Pi$ |
| $\mathrm{Pass}(S,\Pi)$ | Derived (evaluation outcome predicate) | Protocol-pass predicate used to express sensitivity across admissibility-preserving perturbations. | $\mathrm{Pass}(S,\Pi)\in\{0,1\}$ |

| $\widehat{R}_g(S \mid \Pi)$ | Derived (scalar proxy) | Protocol-declared scalar proxy for $R_g$, admissible only if its construction is specified in $\mathcal{R}_\Pi$ and remains subject to disqualification. | $\widehat{R}_g(S \mid \Pi)\in\mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}\;\cup\;\{\varnothing\}$ |
| $\mathrm{RER}_\Pi(S)$ | Threshold (detection) | Candidate re-entry detection threshold under protocol $\Pi$. | $\mathrm{RER}_\Pi(S)\in\{0,1\}$<br>$\mathrm{RER}_\Pi(S)=1 \;:\Longleftrightarrow\; \exists t \in \mathcal{T}_\Pi,\; \mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{RER}}(S,t)$ |
| $\mathcal{R}_\Pi$ | Derived (inference rules) | Declared inference rule set used by protocol $\Pi$. | $\mathcal{R}_\Pi \subset \Pi$ |
| $\Sigma_\Pi(S)$ | Derived (accounting object) | Protocol-accounted irreversibility proxy attributable to evaluation of $S$ under $\Pi$, if included. | $\mathrm{Accounted}_\Pi\!\big(\Sigma_\Pi(S)\big)\in\{0,1\}$ |
| $\mathrm{SnapshotOnly}(\Pi)$ | Derived (protocol disqualifier condition) | Protocol condition indicating a snapshot-only evidence regime. | $\mathrm{SnapshotOnly}(\Pi)\in\{0,1\}$ |
| $\mathrm{Stable}_\Pi(S)$ | Derived (stability predicate) | Stability predicate over a protocol-declared stabilization window. | $\mathrm{Stable}_\Pi(S)\in\{0,1\}$ |
| $\sigma^{\mathrm{cand}}_\Pi(\cdot)$ | Derived (candidate signature predicate) | Protocol-declared candidate signature predicate used for $\mathrm{RER}$ event detection. | $\sigma^{\mathrm{cand}}_\Pi:\mathcal{O}_{R_g}(t)\rightarrow\{0,1\}$ |
| $\sigma_\Pi(S,\theta)$ | Derived (signature predicate) | Protocol-declared signature predicate used in first-order forgery bindings for target $\theta$. | $\theta\in\{\Phi_i,R_g\}$<br>$\sigma_\Pi(S,\theta)\in\{0,1\}$ |

### A2. Lemmata and axiomatic bindings (kernel list)

| Lemma id | Formal binding | Normative consequence |
|---|---|---|
| $\mathrm{A2.1}$ (Axis necessity) | $C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O})=1 \;\Longrightarrow\; \mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(\Phi_i,S)=1 \,\wedge\, \mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(R_g,S)=1$ | Positive classification is admissible only under joint axis admissibility. |
| $\mathrm{A2.2}$ (Non-compensation) | $\mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(\Phi_i,S)\neq 1 \,\vee\, \mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(R_g,S)\neq 1 \;\Longrightarrow\; C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O})\neq 1$ | No axis substitution is admissible, non-admissibility on one axis blocks positivity. |
| $\mathrm{A2.3}$ (Protocol dependence) | $(\Pi_1 \neq \Pi_2) \;\Longrightarrow\; \exists S,\; C_s(S \mid \Pi_1,\mathcal{O}_1) \neq C_s(S \mid \Pi_2,\mathcal{O}_2)$ | CIITR outcomes are protocol-scoped and cannot be asserted as protocol-free system attributes. |
| $\mathrm{A2.4}$ (Disqualification primacy) | $\mathrm{Disq}_\Pi(S)=1 \;\Longrightarrow\; C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O})\neq 1$ | Disqualifiers have binding authority over positive claims. |
| $\mathrm{A2.5}$ (Decidability gating) | $\mathrm{Decidable}_\Pi(\Phi_i,S)=0 \,\vee\, \mathrm{Decidable}_\Pi(R_g,S)=0 \;\Longrightarrow\; C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O})=\varnothing$ | If admissibility cannot be decided under declared rules, the only admissible output is undefined. |
| $\mathrm{A2.6}$ (Snapshot implication) | $\mathrm{SnapshotOnly}(\Pi)=1 \;\Longrightarrow\; \mathrm{Decidable}_\Pi(R_g,S)=0$ | Snapshot-only regimes cannot decide admissibility of the re-entry axis. |
| $\mathrm{A2.7}$ (Temporal invariance nulling) | $\mathrm{TempInv}_\Pi(S)=1 \;\Longrightarrow\; \mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(R_g,S)\neq 1$ | Temporal invariance is a null condition for the re-entry axis under the kernel. |
| $\mathrm{A2.8}$ (Constraint gating for certification relevance) | $\mathrm{CertClaim}_\Pi(S)=1 \;\Longrightarrow\; \mathrm{Accounted}_\Pi\!\big(E_\Pi(S)\big)=1$ | Certification-grade positivity is inadmissible without energy accounting. |
| $\mathrm{A2.9}$ (CPJ definability gating) | $\mathrm{CertClaim}_\Pi(S)=1 \,\wedge\, \mathrm{CPJ}_\Pi(S)\neq\varnothing \;\Longrightarrow\; \widehat{\Phi}_i(S \mid \Pi)\neq\varnothing \,\wedge\, \widehat{R}_g(S \mid \Pi)\neq\varnothing$ | If CPJ is invoked as a gate, its scalar proxies must be declared and defined under the protocol. |

### A4. Disqualifier list (kernel families)

| Disqualifier family | Kernel meaning | Formal trigger / consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Axis null conditions | Non-admissibility on either axis blocks positive classification. | $\mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(\Phi_i,S)\neq 1 \,\vee\, \mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(R_g,S)\neq 1 \;\Longrightarrow\; C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O})\neq 1$ |
| Temporal invariance nulling | Temporal invariance over re-entry observables constitutes a null condition for $R_g$. | $\mathrm{TempInv}_\Pi(S)=1 \;\Longrightarrow\; \mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(R_g,S)\neq 1$ |
| Snapshot regime | Snapshot-only evidence regimes cannot support certification-grade positive claims. | $\mathrm{SnapshotOnly}(\Pi)=1 \;\Longrightarrow\; \mathrm{Decidable}_\Pi(R_g,S)=0$<br>$\mathrm{SnapshotOnly}(\Pi)=1 \;\Longrightarrow\; C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O})\in\{0,\varnothing\}$ |
| First-order forgery | Signature imitation at measurement level without kernel admissibility. | $\mathrm{Forgery}^{(1)}_\Pi(S,\theta)=1 \;\Longrightarrow\; C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O})\neq 1$ |
| Second-order forgery | Protocol gaming and sensitivity to admissibility-preserving perturbations. | $\mathrm{Forgery}^{(2)}_\Pi(S)=1 \;\Longrightarrow\; C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O})\neq 1$ |
| Disqualification primacy | Any protocol-bound disqualifier triggers binding rejection of positive classification. | $\mathrm{Disq}_\Pi(S)=1 \;\Longrightarrow\; C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O})\neq 1$ |
| Audit incompleteness | Missing protocol identity, observable enumeration, rule declaration, or negative control trace forces undefined outcomes and blocks positivity. | $\mathrm{Decidable}_\Pi(\Phi_i,S)=0 \,\vee\, \mathrm{Decidable}_\Pi(R_g,S)=0 \;\Longrightarrow\; C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O})=\varnothing$ |
| Constraint failure for certification relevance | Certification-grade positive claims require energy accounting, and optionally CPJ definability if invoked. | $\mathrm{CertClaim}_\Pi(S)=1 \,\wedge\, \mathrm{Accounted}_\Pi\!\big(E_\Pi(S)\big)=0 \;\Longrightarrow\; \mathrm{CertOutcome}_\Pi(S)\neq \mathrm{CERTIFIED}$<br>$\mathrm{CertClaim}_\Pi(S)=1 \,\wedge\, \mathrm{CPJ}_\Pi(S)=\varnothing \;\Longrightarrow\; \mathrm{CertOutcome}_\Pi(S)\neq \mathrm{CERTIFIED}$ |
